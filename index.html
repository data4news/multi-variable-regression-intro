Step 2 of 7

The Null Model: Just the Mean

Before measuring improvement, we need a baseline. If we predict ȳ = 20.09 MPG for every car — ignoring all predictors — the orange plane shows that level.

Each orange bar is the gap between a car's actual MPG and the mean. Squaring and summing all those gaps gives the total variance we need to explain.

SS_total = Σ (yᵢ − ȳ)² = 1126.05

Step 5 of 7

Explained Variance: SSmodel

The teal bars bridge the gap between what the regression plane predicts and the flat mean plane. This is the variance the model captured.

SS_model = Σ (ŷᵢ − ȳ)²

Teal (explained) + green/red (residual) = original orange bars. That's the core accounting identity:

SS_total = SS_model + SS_residual
1126.05 = 934.88 + 191.17
Step 6 of 7

R²: The Explained Fraction

R² = SS_model / SS_total
R² = 934.88 / 1126.05 = 0.830

From a Flat Mean to a Fitted Plane

The journey from SStotal to SSmodel + SSresidual is the heart of regression analysis. Weight and cylinders together tilt the plane to capture 83% of the variance in fuel economy — leaving only 17% unexplained. Every new predictor we add has a chance to tilt the plane a little further, shrinking the residual bars one more time.
